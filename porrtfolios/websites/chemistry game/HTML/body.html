Sciamachy

Synopsis:




Sciamachy is an adventure-styled, slash choice-based RPG game that follows the old 2D pixelated platform with a four-directional movement. The user is given the opportunity to control their character--a young 12 year-old boy named Peter Lalonde, who finds himself troubled by the constant voice in his head and embarks on a quest to find his true self.
What is Sciamachy?




The title for the game Sciamachy originated from its word's defintion: an argument or conflict with an imaginary opponent. This was meant to symbolize the battles a person faces, and metaphorically speaking, when associating the voices in their heads as an entity to try to fight.
What is the game's purpose?
The game's purpose is to bring awareness and give serious importance to mental illnesses, syndromes or disorders, and common cases such as depression or anxiety. The game does not explicitly state information about the character directly, as it's intention is to let the user practice observational skills and analyzation of events and circumstances.
Game About:
Who do we control?
Peter Lalonde, the character the user plays as, is diagnosed with chronic depression, or better yet, Dysthymia--which is a continuous form of depression that makes a person lose interest in their normal daily activities, have a lack of productivity, feel hopelessness and have extremely low self-esteem and regards for themselves.
Though not explicitly stated, subtext from dialogues within interacting objects in the game subtly hints the multiple symptoms. This includes a problem of insomnia wherein he faces trouble of sleeping at night, traces of previous medicine usages, pessimistic comments on certain objects or events that reflect towards his habits, and a moody personality-switch that turns him passive-aggressive.
His homelife is also evident from the clues left in his house's wake, that despite his negative perspectives, he used to be happy with his family when he was younger.
